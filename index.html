Student researcher + computational biology

Aarnav Bhat

I am a student researcher drawn to the places where biology becomes measurable: fragments of DNA in blood, methylation changes across cancer cohorts, cell states in tumors, and protein structures that can be tested instead of just imagined.

The thread running through my work is validation. I like building models, but I am more interested in whether they survive the uncomfortable parts: clinical controls, held-out data, ablation studies, negative controls, technical confounders, and clear limits on what the results can prove.

About me

I like building things that force ideas to become testable.

I started from a curiosity about disease biology and kept getting pulled toward the computational side because it lets a question become a pipeline: find the data, clean it, decide what a fair test would be, run the model, and then ask what could have fooled it.

MiNAE validation landscape

Why this is more useful than a decorative 3D AUC plot

The point is not to make AUC look dramatic. It is to show that a model score has context: the metric, the validation layer, and whether the result came from one locked split or repeated patient-level testing.

  • Drag or move across the scene to rotate the landscape.
  • Hover a bar to see the metric and reported score.

Selected research systems

The projects are proof of how I work, not the whole landing page.

These are the main research systems behind the portfolio. Each one is kept on its own page so the home page can stay focused on who I am, how I think, and what kind of scientific work I am trying to grow into.

cfDNA fragmentomics

MiNAE: Mutation-independent Nucleosome Architecture Evaluation

A liver cancer fragmentomics framework that learns from physical cfDNA fragment-size patterns instead of expensive tumor mutation sequencing.

  • Tests HCC against cirrhosis and hepatitis B controls
  • Includes ROC/PR curves, SHAP, ablation, and repeated validation
Open project page
Target prioritization

PrISMA-RSC: Pancreatic immunotherapy target ranking

A specificity-aware consensus algorithm for prioritizing pancreatic cancer stromal targets while penalizing nonspecific candidates.

  • Integrates expression, spatial, structure, and control evidence
  • Stress-tests LRRC15 and comparator targets across scoring assumptions
Open project page
Epigenomic diagnostics

DNA methylation biomarkers for lung cancer detection

An AI-driven biomarker study using EGFR, PD-L1, SHOX2, RASSF1A, and PTGER4 methylation patterns across LUAD and LUSC cohorts.

  • 7,240 samples screened, 2,385 final processed samples
  • Gradient boosting model with 0.983 ROC-AUC and 98.2% recall
Open project page
