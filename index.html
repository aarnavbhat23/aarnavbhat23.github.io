Aarnav Bhat

I am a student researcher drawn to the places where biology becomes measurable: fragments of DNA in blood, methylation changes across cancer cohorts, cell states in tumors, and protein structures that can be tested instead of just imagined.

The thread running through my work is validation. I like building models, but I am more interested in whether they survive the uncomfortable parts: clinical controls, held-out data, ablation studies, negative controls, technical confounders, and clear limits on what the results can prove.

How I work

The part I like most is turning a vague idea into a fair test.

My favorite problems sit at the point where biology, data, and communication all matter at once. I am trying to train myself to be curious without being careless, ambitious without overclaiming, and technical without losing the biological question.

Biology first

I try to start with a mechanism or clinical question before touching the model. A clean metric is less meaningful if the biological setup is weak.

Validation as a habit

I like held-out tests, repeated splits, negative controls, ablations, and clear limitations because they make the result harder to fool.

Structure and sequence

I am increasingly interested in how sequence models and protein-structure tools can connect molecular hypotheses to testable designs.

Readable science

I care about writing because a project is not really finished until another person can understand what was done and what was not proven.
